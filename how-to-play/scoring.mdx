Lucky Roll Dice has two distinct score types. Understanding how they interact is key to making good decisions at the table.

## Current Score

Current Score tracks the points you have accumulated **during your active turn**. It starts at 0 when your turn begins and increases with every successful roll.

Current Score is temporary. It disappears in two situations:

- You roll a **1** — Current Score resets to 0 and the turn passes.
- You **hold** — Current Score is added to your Global Score, then resets to 0.

## Global Score

Global Score is your permanent bank. It only increases when you successfully hold, and it never decreases. The first player to reach 100 in their Global Score wins.

Global Score is displayed prominently on each player's side of the screen alongside the Current Score.

## How scores are calculated

The game calculates scores exactly as follows:

**On each roll (2–6):**
```
New Current Score = Current Score + dice result
```

**On hold:**
```
New Global Score = Global Score + Current Score
Current Score = 0
```

**On rolling a 1:**
```
Current Score = 0
(Global Score is unaffected)
```

## Example scenarios

### Successful turn

| Action | Dice result | Current Score | Global Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| Start of turn | — | 0 | 0 |
| Roll | 4 | 4 | 0 |
| Roll | 3 | 7 | 0 |
| Roll | 5 | 12 | 0 |
| Hold | — | 0 | 12 |

The player banks 12 points and passes the turn.

### Rolling a 1

| Action | Dice result | Current Score | Global Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| Start of turn | — | 0 | 12 |
| Roll | 6 | 6 | 12 |
| Roll | 4 | 10 | 12 |
| Roll | 5 | 15 | 12 |
| Roll | 1 | 0 | 12 |

The 15-point Current Score is wiped out. Global Score is unchanged at 12. The turn passes immediately.

### Winning turn

| Action | Dice result | Current Score | Global Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| Start of turn | — | 0 | 87 |
| Roll | 6 | 6 | 87 |
| Roll | 5 | 11 | 87 |
| Roll | 4 | 15 | 87 |
| Hold | — | 0 | 102 |

Global Score reaches 102, which meets the `>= 100` threshold. The game ends and the player wins.

## Strategy tips

<Tip>
  **Hold early when your global score is close to 100.** If your Global Score is at 87 and you have 13 in your Current Score, holding locks in the win. There is no reason to risk a 1.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Consider your opponent's Global Score.** If your opponent is sitting at 95 points and it is their next turn, they only need 5 more to win. It may be worth taking extra risk on your turn to build a large lead before passing.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Each roll after the first carries increasing risk.** The probability of rolling a 1 is always 1 in 6 (about 17%). That probability does not change, but the cost of a 1 grows with each additional roll. A Current Score of 20 is much more painful to lose than a Current Score of 3.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Use small holds to stay safe.** Holding with just 6 or 8 points per turn is slow, but it is safe. Over many turns, steady accumulation can beat a risky opponent who keeps losing their Current Score to unlucky 1s.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does rolling a 1 affect my Global Score?">
    No. Rolling a 1 only resets your Current Score to 0. Your Global Score is never reduced under any circumstance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I win by rolling, without holding?">
    No. The win condition is checked only after a hold. Even if your Current Score would push you past 100, you must click Hold for the win to be registered.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if both players reach 100 on the same turn?">
    This is not possible. Only the active player can hold, and the game ends immediately when their Global Score reaches 100. The other player does not get a turn.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a maximum score?">
    No. The win condition triggers at exactly 100 or above. In theory a player could bank more than 100 in a single hold, but the game ends as soon as the threshold is met.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
