Lucky Roll Dice has a small set of rules that are quick to learn. This page covers everything from starting a game to the win condition.

## Starting the game

Click **New Game** to begin. This resets all four score displays to 0 and assigns the red dot turn indicator to Player 1.

<Note>
  Before **New Game** is clicked, the turn indicator dot has not been assigned to either player. Clicking Roll Dice or Hold in this state does nothing.
</Note>

## Flow of a turn

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check the turn indicator">
    The red dot next to a player's name marks whose turn it is. If it is your turn, you have two choices: Roll Dice or Hold.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Roll the dice">
    Click **Roll Dice**. The die shows a random value from 1 to 6.

    - If the result is **2–6**, that number is added to your Current Score. You may roll again or hold.
    - If the result is **1**, your Current Score resets to 0 and your turn ends immediately. The dot moves to your opponent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hold (optional)">
    At any point on your turn, click **Hold** instead of rolling. Your Current Score is added to your Global Score, your Current Score resets to 0, and the turn passes to your opponent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Win the game">
    After holding, if your Global Score reaches or exceeds 100, the game ends and you are declared the winner. The page reloads automatically to allow a rematch.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Rolling a 1

<Warning>
  Rolling a 1 is the harshest outcome in the game. Your entire Current Score for that turn is lost — it resets to 0 — and the turn immediately passes to your opponent. Any points you accumulated during the turn are gone and cannot be recovered.
</Warning>

This rule is the source of all the risk in the game. A player sitting on 20 Current Score points has everything to lose by rolling one more time.

## Holding

When you hold:

1. Your Current Score is added to your Global Score.
2. Your Current Score resets to 0.
3. The turn dot moves to your opponent.

Holding is the only way to permanently bank points. Points in your Current Score do not count toward the win condition until you hold.

## Win condition

<Note>
  The first player whose Global Score reaches **100 or more points** after holding wins the game. The threshold is `>= 100`, so exactly 100 is sufficient. The win is checked immediately after the hold action completes, not after a roll.
</Note>

When a player wins, a browser alert announces the winner and the page reloads, resetting everything for a new match.

## Rules summary

| Situation | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Roll 2–6 | Add to Current Score, continue your turn |
| Roll 1 | Current Score resets to 0, turn passes |
| Hold | Current Score moves to Global Score, turn passes |
| Global Score ≥ 100 after holding | Player wins, game resets |
