Lucky Roll Dice is a two-player browser game played on a single shared screen — no accounts, no downloads, no network required. Both players take turns on the same device, competing to be the first to bank 100 points.

The game is built around a single six-sided die and three simple controls. The tension comes from a classic push-your-luck dilemma: keep rolling to build a bigger score, or hold now before you lose everything.

## The goal

The first player to accumulate **100 or more points** in their Global Score wins the game. Points are earned by rolling the die and banking your turn score before rolling a 1.

## Controls

<Columns cols={3}>
  <Card title="New Game" icon="rotate-right">
    Resets all scores to 0 and gives Player 1 the first turn. Click this at the start of every match.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Roll Dice" icon="dice">
    Rolls the die and adds the result to your Current Score. Rolling a 1 wipes your Current Score and ends your turn immediately.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Hold" icon="hand">
    Banks your Current Score into your Global Score, resets your Current Score to 0, and passes the turn to your opponent.
  </Card>
</Columns>

## Turn indicator

A small red dot appears next to the active player's name, showing whose turn it is. The dot moves to the other player when a turn ends — either by holding or by rolling a 1.

<Tip>
  Before clicking **New Game**, neither player has the red dot indicator. The dot is only assigned to Player 1 once the game begins. If you try to roll or hold before starting a new game, nothing will happen.
</Tip>

## Score types

Each player has two score displays:

| Score | What it tracks |
|---|---|
| **Current Score** | Points accumulated during the current turn. Lost entirely if you roll a 1. |
| **Global Score** | Permanently banked points that persist across turns. This is the score that counts toward winning. |

Current Score is the risk. Global Score is the reward. The only way to move points from one to the other is to Hold.

## The push-your-luck decision

Every roll after the first is a gamble. You might have 18 points sitting in your Current Score — enough to pull ahead — but one bad roll wipes it all out and hands the turn to your opponent. Holding feels safe, but your opponent could be one big turn away from winning.

This tension — roll again or hold now — is the heart of the game.
